Matcha Cultivation

The art behind premium green tea

The posters also explain the traditional process behind matcha quality, from the tea plant to the final fine powder.

How matcha is cultivated

1

Cultivation

Camellia sinensis is cultivated in well-known tea-growing regions such as Uji, Nishio, Shizuoka, Aichi and Kagoshima.

2

Shade growing

About 3–4 weeks before harvest, the plants are shaded. This supports chlorophyll development and contributes to umami and smoothness.

3

Harvest

For ceremonial-quality matcha, the youngest and most tender leaves are selected, especially from the first harvest.

4

Processing into Tencha

The leaves are steamed, dried, and destemmed before they become tencha, the base material for matcha.

5

Stone milling

Tencha is slowly ground in stone mills into a very fine powder, preserving texture and character.

Why premium matcha stands out

  • Rich green colour from careful shading and leaf quality
  • Natural sweetness and soft umami profile
  • L-Theanine associated with calm focus
  • Fine silky texture from careful milling
  • Less bitterness when high-quality young leaves are used

Hojicha

Roasted green tea with a warm, nutty profile

Your Hojicha poster adds another strong product story to the brand — a roasted tea option with a cosy, evening-friendly identity.

Why Hojicha fits the brand

  • Naturally lower caffeine profile
  • Warm, roasted, nutty flavour
  • Comforting and soothing tea character
  • Easy to position as a relaxing alternative to stronger drinks
  • Can support an evening or wellness-style offering

How Hojicha is made

1

Cultivation

Green tea plants are grown and prepared in the usual tea-growing process.

2

Harvest

The leaves are harvested and dried before roasting.

3

Roasting

The leaves are roasted at high temperature, giving Hojicha its brown colour and signature aroma.

4

Cooling & sorting

The tea is cooled, sorted, and prepared for brewing.

5

Brewing

With hot water, it becomes a smooth roasted tea with a naturally comforting taste.
